──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 POWER RAILS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  3.3 V  (Pin 1)  ──┬──→ DHT11 VCC
                    └──→ Push button (one leg)

  5.0 V  (Pin 2)  ──┬──→ HC-SR04 VCC
                    └──→ (positive rail on breadboard)

  GND    (Pin 6)  ──┬──→ DHT11 GND
                    ├──→ HC-SR04 GND
                    ├──→ LED cathode (through 220 Ω resistor)
                    ├──→ Buzzer GND
                    └──→ Push button pull-down resistor (10 kΩ to GND)

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 DHT11 TEMPERATURE & HUMIDITY SENSOR
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  DHT11 Pin  →  Connection
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  VCC        →  3.3 V  (Pi Pin 1)
  GND        →  GND    (Pi Pin 6)
  DATA       →  GPIO 4 (Pi Pin 7)

  Note: A 10 kΩ pull-up resistor is recommended between DATA and VCC.
        Many breakout boards include this; bare sensors need it on the breadboard.

  Why GPIO 4?
  The Adafruit_DHT library uses bit-banging (precise timing) on the data pin.
  GPIO 4 works reliably for this purpose on all Pi models.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 HC-SR04 ULTRASONIC DISTANCE SENSOR
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  HC-SR04 Pin  →  Connection
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  VCC          →  5 V      (Pi Pin 2)
  GND          →  GND      (Pi Pin 6)
  TRIG         →  GPIO 17  (Pi Pin 11)   ← 3.3 V output safe
  ECHO         →  Voltage divider → GPIO 27 (Pi Pin 13)

  ⚠ IMPORTANT – Voltage Divider on ECHO line
  ───────────────────────────────────────────
  The HC-SR04 ECHO output is 5 V logic.
  The Raspberry Pi GPIO inputs are 3.3 V MAX.  Exceeding this will
  permanently damage the GPIO pin.

  Voltage divider circuit (reduces 5 V → 3.3 V):

    HC-SR04 ECHO ──┬── 1 kΩ ──┬── GPIO 27
                   │           │
                              2 kΩ
                               │
                              GND

  Calculation:
    V_out = V_in × R2 / (R1 + R2)
          = 5 V  × 2000 / (1000 + 2000)
          = 5 V  × 0.667
          = 3.33 V  ✓  (within GPIO 3.3 V tolerance)

  Why GPIO 17 for TRIG?
  TRIG only receives a 3.3 V output from the Pi (GPIO is 3.3 V output).
  The HC-SR04 TRIG input works with 3.3 V logic, so no level shifting needed.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 LED  (status indicator)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  GPIO 22 (Pi Pin 15) ── LED anode
  LED cathode ── 220 Ω resistor ── GND

  Why 220 Ω?
  GPIO high = 3.3 V.  A typical red/green LED has Vf ≈ 2.0–2.2 V.
  I = (3.3 − 2.1) / 220 = 5.5 mA  (safe for GPIO, max is 16 mA per pin)

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 BUZZER  (active, 3.3 V compatible)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  GPIO 23 (Pi Pin 16) ── Buzzer + terminal
  Buzzer − terminal ── GND

  Note: Use an ACTIVE buzzer (contains its own oscillator).
        A passive buzzer requires PWM; active buzzer just needs a DC signal.
        If buzzer draws > 20 mA, use a 2N2222 NPN transistor to drive it
        from 5 V rail via GPIO control signal.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 PUSH BUTTON
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  One leg  →  3.3 V  (Pi Pin 1)
  Other leg →  GPIO 24 (Pi Pin 18)  AND  10 kΩ resistor → GND

  When NOT pressed:  GPIO 24 pulled LOW through 10 kΩ  → reads 0 / False
  When pressed:      GPIO 24 connected to 3.3 V         → reads 1 / True

  The software also enables the internal Pi pull-down (GPIO.PUD_DOWN)
  as a secondary safeguard.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 BREADBOARD LAYOUT GUIDE  (left-to-right)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Power rails:
    Red  (+) rail ── 3.3 V (for DHT11, button)
    Blue (−) rail ── GND   (common ground)

  Section A (rows 1–10):   DHT11 sensor + pull-up resistor
  Section B (rows 11–20):  HC-SR04 + voltage divider (R1=1kΩ, R2=2kΩ)
  Section C (rows 21–28):  LED + 220 Ω resistor
  Section D (rows 29–32):  Active buzzer
  Section E (rows 33–38):  Push button + 10 kΩ pull-down
